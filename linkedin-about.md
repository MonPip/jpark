Right out of college, I spent a year helping at-risk youth transition out of "the system" and into "the real world." During a training on the emotional challenges our residents faced, the instructor said, "It's not rocket science." He was right, it was harder. But that's exactly the kind of work I signed up for: jump into the uncomfortable, figure it out, and grow into someone who can actually help.

I'm no longer in that world, but the instinct stuck. Every role since has been the same challenge at a different scale: walk into something messy, bring clarity, and ship. I lead with discovery, prototype to test ideas, and align teams around evidence instead of opinions.
